"Form follows function
- that has been misunderstood.
Form and function should be one,
joined in a spiritual union."

Frank Llyod Wright

I believe that aesthetics has purposes of it's own, more than being a by-product of function. It creates the first impressions of beauty & delight, drives curiosity and tells our gut reactions whether to try out or abandon a product or not. It differentiates the product by giving character inside it's breed. It can be a way to solve for people of different tastes. Imagine how boring and monotonous would be the world around us, if everything was designed purely on functional objectives.

All these holds true in the digital world too, where we tend to classify UX and UI as discrete entities. Can a design be good, without these working in harmony?
