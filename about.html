Form follows function
Form + function

When it comes to design, I believe that aesthetics has a purpose of it's own, rather than being a by-product of functionality. Imagine how boring and characterless would be the world around us, if everything were designed purely on functional objectives. Humans are hard-wired for visual input, and their first impressions form in a very short span of time. It's the aesthetics that drives these impressions of beauty, delight and excitement, and tell their gut reactions to continue or abandon a product. Form and function is equally important, and have to work hand in hand.
