"Form follows function
- that has been misunderstood.
Form and function should be one,
joined in a spiritual union."

Frank Llyod Wright

When it comes to design, I believe that aesthetics has a purpose of it's own, more than being a by-product of functionality. Imagine how boring and characterless would be the world around us, if everything were designed purely on functional objectives. Humans are hard-wired for visual input, and our first impressions of a product/design form in a very short span of time. It's the aesthetics that drives these impressions of beauty, delight and excitement, and tell our gut reactions to continue or abandon a product. Form and function are equally important and have to work hand in hand.
