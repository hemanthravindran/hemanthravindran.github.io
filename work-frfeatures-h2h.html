A competition and community engagement feature for rap students, inspired from the real world rap battles

How it works

Students entering into the rap battle section can either start battling with an opponent, or just watch & vote for others' battles. When a student enters a battle, they are matched with an opponent and gets ready for dissing each other (which we call battle). A diss means a video of the student dissing the opponent. They have 24 hours time to diss each other as many times they want. Once the time is expired, their sequence of diss videos is stitched as a streak and published for voting. Other rap students can watch & vote the streak for 48 hours, and then the winner is announced.

Battles home

The home page for rap battles is mainly a feed of all ongoing and completed battles. The primary action is to start a new battle and the secondary option is to vote on others' battles.

Opponent matching

Once a user starts a new battle from the homepage, another user is matched based on the user's battling rank (win/attended ratio). The closest one is selected as an opponent. The user can visit the profile and chat with opponent if they wish to, and then start the battle form their end. If they prefer to chose a different opponent, they can retry the matching process.

Start dissing

Once the opponent is matched, the user is asked to upload or shoot their first diss. The upload screen gives an idea of how the entire streak is going to look like

Shooting from camera has features like visual metronome to help them keep up with the tempo. Also a countdown indication of 30 seconds is shown

Once the video is shot or uploaded, it becomes the first diss in the streak. Until the opponent disses back the user, they can't do anything but wait.

The streak

For a battle to be able to get published, the streak needs to have atleast 1 diss from each side. The validity of streak creation is 24 hours and the participants can upload as many disses they want during this time. A color coded pagination is associated with a streak for easily relate with each participant.
